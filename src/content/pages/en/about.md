Hi, I'm Lu Jiaqi.

AI Engineer & Creator.

Drawing on my knowledge and life experience, I use AI-era tools to express myself and continuously create value that makes the world better.

I joined Oriental Selection (东方甄选) as a Management Trainee, serving as Customer Service Supervisor. I worked deeply with the front-line CS team, helping the team I managed maintain a user satisfaction rate above 95% and deliver quality service. As CS Supervisor, I built a 20+ person cross-platform comment optimization team from scratch, raising the user comment reply pass rate from 80% to 99.7% — delivering a better support experience.

I joined the United Nations Industrial Development Organization (UNIDO) Shanghai Global Science, Technology and Innovation Center as an intern. I co-organized a 50+ person on-site dual-carbon education event, followed up on 5 center meetings, and wrote multiple press releases — supporting the smooth running of the center's daily work.

While researching a case for my graduation thesis, I discovered issues at the garbage disposal station near the student dormitory area — and started my own "small step to make the world better." I interviewed nearby students, observed the station's operations on-site, gathered first-hand material, and combined my major's knowledge into several solutions. Through the school's official feedback channel, I reported the problems and proposed fixes. By the time I graduated that June, the original simple disposal station had been upgraded with noise reduction and disinfection equipment — the surrounding world had become genuinely a little better.

After receiving the Minsheng Yan Keqiang Higher Education Scholarship, inspired by the "kindness relay" idea, I decided to donate part of it to a rural primary school. I called on fellow scholarship recipients to join in. We searched through many projects, faced many rejections and failures, and eventually chose to donate streetlights to Jiangtuan Primary School in Dongshan Township, Bama Yao Autonomous County, Guangxi. Later, inspired by what we did, our teacher also donated lights. As of 2025, four streetlights have been lit on the way to school for children in the mountains of Guangxi because of me — the world is different because of me, with one more lit road on the journey home.
